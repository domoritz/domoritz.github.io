--- layout: publication year: 2023 title: "Average Estimates in Line Graphs are Biased Toward Areas of Higher Variability" highlight: true authors: - Dominik Moritz - Lace Padilla - Francis Nguyen - Steven Franconeri type: - Conference tags: - Visualization - Bias - Perception venue: VIS venue_location: Melbourne venue_url: https://ieeevis.org/year/2023/welcome venue_tags: - VIS pdf: https://www.domoritz.de/papers/2023-LineBias-VIS.pdf link: https://osf.io/aupbk/ slides: https://www.slideshare.net/domoritz/2023-vis-line-bias arxiv: "2308.03903" doi: "10.1109/TVCG.2023.3326589" awards: - Best Paper Honorable Mention --- We investigate variability overweighting, a previously undocumented bias in line graphs, where estimates of average value are biased toward areas of higher variability in that line. We found this effect across two preregistered experiments with 140 and 420 participants. These experiments also show that the bias is reduced when using a dot encoding of the same series. We can model the bias with the average of the data series and the average of the points drawn along the line. This bias might arise because higher variability leads to stronger weighting in the average calculation, either due to the longer line segments (even though those segments contain the same number of data values) or line segments with higher variability being otherwise more visually salient. Understanding and predicting this bias is important for visualization design guidelines, recommendation systems, and tool builders, as the bias can adversely affect estimates of averages and trends.
