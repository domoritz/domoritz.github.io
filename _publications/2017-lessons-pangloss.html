--- layout: publication authors: - Dominik Moritz - Danyel Fisher pdf: https://www.domoritz.de/papers/2017-Pangloss-Uncertainty.pdf tags: - Uncertainty title: "Lessons from Pangloss: User Encounters with Uncertainty" summary: Tools need to better support analysts who work with approximate data. type: - Workshop venue: Designing for Uncertainty in HCI at CHI venue_location: Denver, CO, USA venue_tags: - CHI - Designing for Uncertainty venue_url: http://visualization.ischool.uw.edu/hci_uncertainty/ year: 2017 --- Pangloss implements "Optimistic Visualization", a method that gives analysts confidence to use approximate results for exploratory data analysis. In this position paper, we outline some ways in which user experiences with an approximate visualization system did not match analysts' intuitions. These observations have implications for the design of future systems that expose uncertainty to users.
